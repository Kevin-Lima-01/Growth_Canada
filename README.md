# Growth Diagnostics for Canada

This repository contains the final project and supporting codes developed as part of a **Macroeconomics II** course focused on **economic growth**. The study performs a **Growth Diagnostics** analysis for **Canada**, identifying binding constraints to economic growth. The analysis draws on data from the **World Bank** and the **Bank of Canada**, using **R** to construct the database, generate visualizations, and estimate the key relationships.

## Research Objective

The central goal is to apply the **Growth Diagnostics framework** (a la Hausmann, Rodrik, and Velasco) to the Canadian economy. Rather than assuming a one-size-fits-all explanation, the methodology systematically investigates which specific factors represent the most severe obstacles to sustained economic expansion.

## Key Constraints Investigated

The analysis focuses on three potential binding constraints to Canadian economic growth:

- 🔹 **Economic Freedom:** To what extent do regulatory and institutional barriers limit entrepreneurial activity and productivity growth?
- 🔹 **Tax Burden:** Does the level and structure of taxation distort investment decisions and labor supply in a way that constrains growth?
- 🔹 **Return on Investment:** Are low or declining returns to capital signaling deeper structural bottlenecks in the Canadian economy?

## Methodology

The project follows the standard Growth Diagnostics decision tree approach, proceeding through the following steps:

1. **Data Compilation:** Aggregating macroeconomic indicators from the World Bank database and complementary series from the Bank of Canada.
2. **Base Construction:** Cleaning, transforming, and structuring time-series data for analysis in R.
3. **Constraint Identification:** Applying diagnostic tests and generating comparative visualizations to isolate the most binding constraint(s) among the three candidates.
4. **Policy Implications:** Drawing conclusions on which reforms would be most effective in alleviating Canada's growth constraints.

## Data Sources

- **World Bank:** World Development Indicators (WDI)
- **Bank of Canada:** Key macroeconomic and financial series
